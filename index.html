introduction

Virat Kohli, born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, is widely regarded as one of the greatest cricketers of all time. Known for his aggressive batting style, impeccable technique, and extraordinary fitness, Kohli has carved out an illustrious career in international cricket. He began his journey in cricket at a young age, joining the West Delhi Cricket Academy when he was just nine years old. His talent was evident early on, and he quickly ascended through the ranks of youth cricket. Kohli's leadership qualities were also apparent, as he captained the Indian team to victory in the 2008 Under-19 Cricket World Cup. His performance in domestic cricket and the IPL (Indian Premier League) further solidified his reputation as a future star, leading to his debut for the Indian national team in 2008.

Over the years, Kohli has amassed a plethora of records and accolades, establishing himself as a dominant force in world cricket. His aggressive and consistent batting has made him a cornerstone of the Indian batting lineup across all formats. Kohli's fitness regimen and work ethic are often cited as benchmarks for aspiring cricketers. Beyond his on-field achievements, he has also made significant contributions off the field, including his involvement in various philanthropic activities and his commitment to promoting fitness and healthy living. Kohli's passion for the game and his relentless pursuit of excellence have made him an inspiration for millions of cricket fans around the globe.

Career Start

Virat Kohli’s professional cricket career began with a strong foundation in domestic cricket. He first made waves in the cricketing world as a member of the Delhi Under-15 team in 2002, where he quickly became the leading run-scorer in the 2002-03 Polly Umrigar Trophy. His stellar performances continued, and he progressed to the Under-17 level, playing in the Vijay Merchant Trophy during the 2004-05 season. His consistent performance earned him a spot in the India Under-19 team, and his leadership qualities soon became apparent

Kohli’s major breakthrough came in 2008 when he captained the Indian team to victory in the Under-19 Cricket World Cup held in Malaysia. His leadership and crucial knocks throughout the tournament caught the attention of the national selectors. Later that year, he was selected for the Indian ODI squad, making his debut against Sri Lanka in August 2008. Batting as an opener, Kohli scored a decent 12 runs, but it was clear that he had the potential to become a mainstay in the team. His first notable performance came in his fourth ODI, where he scored a vital 54 runs, helping India chase down a target against Sri Lanka. This marked the beginning of Kohli’s journey in international cricket, where he would soon evolve into one of the sport’s biggest icons.

Major Achievements

Batting Milestones

  • First ODI Century (2009)

    Kohli scored his maiden ODI century against Sri Lanka on December 24, 2009, in Kolkata, which was crucial in cementing his place in the national team.

  • Fastest to 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, 11,000, and 12,000 ODI Runs

    Kohli holds the record for being the fastest batsman to reach these milestones in One Day Internationals (ODIs), surpassing cricket legends like Sachin Tendulkar.

  • First T20I Century (2021):

    He scored his first century in T20 Internationals against England on March 12, 2021, becoming one of the few players to have centuries in all three formats of the game.

Captaincy Achievements

  • 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup:

    Kohli was a vital part of the Indian squad that won the 2011 World Cup. He scored a century in his World Cup debut match, becoming the first Indian to do so.

  • 2013 ICC Champions Trophy:

    As the vice-captain, he played a significant role in India’s triumph, contributing consistently throughout the tournament.

  • Test Captaincy:

    Under his captaincy, India became the top-ranked Test team in the world. He led India to their first Test series win in Australia in the 2018-19 season.

  • ODI and T20 Captaincy:

    Kohli led India to several bilateral series wins, including historic victories in South Africa, Australia, and New Zealand. He also captained the Indian team to the final of the 2017 ICC Champions Trophy.

Records and Awards

  • ICC Awards:

    Kohli has been named the ICC ODI Player of the Year in 2012, 2017, and 2018, the ICC Test Player of the Year in 2018, and won the prestigious Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for ICC Cricketer of the Year in 2017 and 2018.

  • BCCI Awards:

    He has received the Polly Umrigar Award for International Cricketer of the Year on multiple occasions, including in 2011-12, 2014-15, 2015-16, 2016-17, and 2017-18.

  • National Honours:

    Kohli was awarded the Arjuna Award in 2013, the Padma Shri in 2017, and India’s highest sporting honour, the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna, in 2018.

  • Wisden Leading Cricketer in the World:

    He was named the Leading Cricketer in the World by Wisden in 2016, 2017, 2018, and 2019.
